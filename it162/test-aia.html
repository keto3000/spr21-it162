AIA (Audience, Issues and Approach)

Client's name/final decision maker: Rachel Taylor & Naomi Taylor-Miller
Client's business/website name: TaylorRenovations.com (placeholder)
New site or redesign: Brand new website
Type of website: Residential remodeling & renovation: Identity, Marketing, Sales
Client Goals: Establish an online presence, create brand ID, showcase porfolio of work, and attract new clientele
AUDIENCE ISSUES/NEEDS APPROACH/SOLVE
  • Audience Group #1: Moderate income, working. Middle class residential homes.

  • Age: ~35-55, partnered, w/family.

  • Household Income: $50k-$150k.

  • Home value: $100k-$499k.

  • Type of Remodel Jobs: kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, deck, landscaping, roofing, painting.

  • Contracting Experience Level: First time remodeling/ minor exp hiring contractor.

  • Tech/Computer Experience Level: Savvy, mobile, tablet, desktop user.
  • First time remodeling home.

  • Tends to have smaller budget requiring either cheaper materials, or more cost-efficient space design.

  • Busy, on-the go lifestyle,job,family needs.

  • Need for micro-scheduling around homeowner job/family is usual for this group.
  • Branding ID & SEO marketing tags are crucial for new customers/future referrals.

  • SMO links: Facebook, Yelp.

  • HOMEPAGE needs strong CALL TO ACTION. Easy for potential buyer to schedule a call, make appointment, register their email, request a brochure.

  • Successful ‘WOW’ PHOTOS.

  • SERVICES PAGE should be simple & include services offered,special problems solved, areas of service, & a photo of friendly crew interacting with a client.

  • LEADS Capture for new customers: Email signup form "For More Information".

  • FAQ or TOP 10 QUESTIONS page.

  • Monetize website. Create adverts/links page for favored local vendors to help.

  • Future: Client Forms Section-Downloadable pdfs: Ex: Initial CLIENT SURVEY; Explain seasonal & usual timing of subcontractors; materials procurement & present a ‘realistic’ project plan to buyer.
  • Audience Group #2: Working/Retired. Manufactured homes.

  • Age:25-40+, 65+ single/partnered.

  • Household Income: 50k-100k.

  • Home value: $30k-100k.

  • Type of Remodel Jobs: Kitchen, bath, bedroom, decking, roofing, landscaping.

  • Contracting Experience Level: Some/moderate exp hiring contractors for small-medium jobs.

  • Tech/Computer Experience Level: Mainly mobile, desktop.
  • Trust is very important to this group.

  • Strict budget. Prefers quality. Willing to cut scope of project or use medium-price tiered products, if necessary.

  • Renovations for needed repair, govt code, storm-proofing.

  • Usually accommodating & friendly group.
  • Establish clear communication, easy to use navigation with simple, direct link names.

  • Easy to find links or potential buyer to schedule a call, make appointment, register their email, request a brochure.

  • ABOUT US page: Strong mission statement. Client’s back story-keep it brief. More CREW IN ACTION ‘pro’ photos here. Buyer reassurance: This group seeks client’s assurance that they understand this buyer group and its need. Stress client’s ability to handle entire project as general contractor. Take guesswork out of it & show willingness to help buyer at every step of process.

  • Courtesy Links & PDFs section: Picking colors; How to measure; Budget-conscious options to increase property value; Cost saving energy renovations; Assistive Remodeling options for Life Transitions.
  • Audience Group #3: High income. Luxury Residential homes.

  • Age: 50s-70. CEOs/retired. Single/partnered.

  • Household Income: 250k -775k+

  • Home value: $500k-$999k+

  • Type of Remodel Jobs: Luxury, big ticket jobs 50k+: kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, decking, extensions, landscaping, roofing, painting.

  • Contracting Experience Level: Experienced. Has frequently hired contractors- some good, some bad.

  • Tech/Computer Experience Level: Savvy, mobile, tablet, desktop user.
  • This group can be high pressure &demanding of detail or low key & relaxed.

  • Expects efficient timetables for project, minimal intrusion of on-the-job subcontractors in personal home space.

  • Can be fussy on issues that normally are usual & customary practices for contracting jobs.
  • SEO tags are important to this group. Highlight client’s reputation, licensing, credentials.

  • Client List & Testimonials should include before/after pics. Website should include proven successful in this luxury range.

  • Mobile-first website design is very important to this group

  • Determine buyer’s “real budget” expectations and establish a workable range.
